Quick Tip

Here's something I use a lot at the command line — especially handy when you make a typing error. It's one of many keyboard shortcuts but definitely worth memorizing. Are you ready? Here it is:

[Ctrl]+[A]

That will take your cursor to the start of the current command, before you press Enter. You can remember it by recalling that A is the start of the Roman alphabet, and by association represents the start of the command you're typing. So when would you use it? Let's say I've just typed this command:

fmpeg -i myvideo.mp4 -acodec libvorbis -aq 5 -ac 2 -qmax 25 -threads 2 myvideo.webm

Notice the typo? fmpeg should be ffmpeg so I just hit [Ctrl]+[A] to jump to the start of the line and correct it, saving lots of cursor pressing. Very simple but a big timesaver over the years.
